## Design Context

### Users

**Primary hybrid audience** with progressive disclosure as the core UX principle:

- **Layer 1 — Worried everyday user**: Someone who received a suspicious WhatsApp forward and needs immediate, calm reassurance. They need a verdict in under 3 seconds of reading. Interface must feel approachable, not intimidating.
- **Layer 2 — Journalist / researcher**: Wants to drill into forensic evidence — Grad-CAM++ heatmaps, EXIF tables, LLM-generated breakdown cards. They expand beyond the verdict card.

**Emotional goal**: "I am in safe, highly capable hands. The answer is clear, and the proof is absolute."

The interface must deliver instant clarity at the surface, then reward deeper exploration without ever feeling overwhelming.

### Brand Personality

**Forensic · Premium · Calm**

- **Forensic**: Grounded in unassailable evidence. Every element earns its place by communicating precision. Data is structured, not dumped.
- **Premium**: Feels like a high-end enterprise tool (Stripe / Vercel / Loti AI tier). Smooth scroll-triggered motion, intentional whitespace, nothing feels cheap.
- **Calm**: Never alarmist. Even a "Fake" verdict is delivered with the clinical detachment of a lab report. No flashing sirens, no heavy red warning banners.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Light theme.** Crisp warm-white and off-white surfaces with deep charcoal-indigo typography as the drama. Not the flat brightness of generic SaaS — the specific elevated lightness of Loti AI: high-contrast type on white, full-bleed section blocks that alternate between white and very subtle tinted backgrounds, with deep rich accent colors (dark teal, deep indigo) used as the authority signal.

**Motion language** (Loti AI / Framer reference):
- Scroll-triggered staggered reveals — sections, cards, and text blocks animate in as the user scrolls down
- Smooth parallax on hero imagery or background elements
- Sections that "lock" briefly as you scroll past (scroll-snapping or scroll-linked transforms)
- The 3D layer scan animation on the analyze page is the signature moment (see below)

**Color palette approach**:
- Background: `oklch(98% 0.005 260)` — warm white with the faintest indigo tint, never pure white
- Surface: `oklch(100% 0 0)` — pure white cards to contrast against the tinted background
- Primary text: `oklch(18% 0.02 260)` — deep charcoal-indigo, not pure black
- Secondary text: `oklch(45% 0.015 260)` — cool mid-gray with indigo tint
- Brand accent: `oklch(42% 0.18 210)` — deep teal, used sparingly for CTAs and links
- Semantic: green `oklch(52% 0.16 155)` / amber `oklch(68% 0.18 65)` / crimson `oklch(48% 0.22 25)`

**Typography direction** (font selection to be finalized per procedure):
- Display/heading: a geometric or humanist sans with strong weight contrast — something with character, not Inter. Should feel like a lab instrument manual meets editorial magazine.
- Body: a refined, highly legible sans — warm, not sterile

**References**:
- [Loti AI](https://www.lotiai.com/) — light, premium, bold type, animated detection visualizations, scroll motion
- [Framer reference](https://magnificent-slides-050584.framer.app/) — motion language, scroll effects, type weight contrast, section pacing

**Anti-references**:
- NOT VirusTotal: no chaotic data walls, no hacker-terminal aesthetic
- NOT generic Material/Bootstrap admin: no flat white-label SaaS look, explicitly override old Material Blue direction
- NOT sensationalist: no aggressive warning banners, no alarmist color overuse
- NOT dark mode with glowing accents: that aesthetic has been set aside in favor of the light premium direction

**The one thing users remember**: The **3D layer animation** on the analyze page. When a file is dropped, 5–7 semi-transparent clones of the uploaded image stack in 3D space (`transform: rotateX(60deg) perspective(800px)`), with a glowing scanning line sweeping across them. Each layer = a different forensic pass (artifact scan, face mesh, heatmap overlay, compression analysis). On a light background, the layers cast soft shadows and the scanner glow is a precise teal. This communicates deep, multi-layered analysis before the verdict appears.

### Design Principles

1. **Progressive disclosure over information dump** — The verdict card is the whole story for casual users. Evidence panels, heatmaps, EXIF data, and LLM breakdowns expand on demand. Never force depth on someone who wants the answer.

2. **Motion as narrative** — Every scroll reveal, panel entrance, and score meter fill tells the story of "thorough analysis." The 3D scan animation is not decoration — it is the product's primary trust mechanism. Loti AI reference: motion communicates competence.

3. **Contrast does the work** — Light theme premium feel comes from extreme type weight contrast (heavy display headlines against light body copy) and extreme value contrast (near-black type on near-white backgrounds). Not from color overload.

4. **Accents are rare and semantic** — Deep teal for brand/interactive, amber for warning/suspicious, crimson for fake/manipulated, green for authentic. These are the only saturated colors in the system. Everything else is the tinted neutral scale. Overuse destroys the signal.

5. **Sections breathe, then snap to attention** — Generous vertical spacing between sections with scroll-triggered reveals. The rhythm should feel like turning pages in a well-designed annual report: open, spacious, then suddenly bold.
